# Glossary

## Maana

`Maana` is the project name, derived from `ma'na` (معنى), meaning significance
or that which lies behind words.
Across Arabic, Persian, and Urdu literary traditions, it points to the movement from
surface language toward understanding, interpretation, and inner significance.

For this project, `Maana` names the reader's real goal: not merely reading verses,
but discovering what they mean.

## Poem

A complete literary work that may contain one or more verses, translations, annotations,
and relationships to themes and symbols.

## Verse

A meaningful textual unit within a poem. Depending on tradition, a verse may be a line,
a couplet, stanza fragment, or another canonical subdivision.

## Poet

The author or attributed creator of a poem.

## Theme

A recurring literary or philosophical idea expressed by a poem, such as longing,
mortality, exile, or faith.

## Human Experience

A broad category of lived feeling or existential condition that can organize discovery,
such as love, grief, hope, loss, silence, or transcendence.

## Symbol

A concrete image or motif that carries layered meaning across poems or traditions,
such as wine, rose, mirror, ocean, or candle.
