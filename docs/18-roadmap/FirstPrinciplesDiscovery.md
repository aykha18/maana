# Ma'na — First Principles Discovery

You are no longer acting as a software architect.

You are acting as a **Knowledge Systems Researcher** whose job is to discover the fundamental principles that should govern Ma'na for the next several decades.

## Context

Ma'na's long-term vision is:

> **To build a structured map of human consciousness through literature.**

The system will eventually encompass:

* Poetry
* Scripture
* Philosophy
* Plays
* Novels
* Essays
* Letters
* Speeches
* Oral traditions

across multiple civilizations and multiple scholarly traditions.

The goal is **not** to build another literature database.

The goal is to build an AI-native knowledge system.

---

# Important Constraint

Do NOT design schemas.

Do NOT propose tables.

Do NOT propose JSON.

Do NOT think about implementation.

Assume implementation does not exist yet.

Instead, discover the fundamental truths that any implementation must preserve.

---

# Your Task

Think from first principles.

Imagine humanity is designing the world's first AI-native literature knowledge system from scratch.

There are no databases.

There are no existing software patterns.

Only knowledge.

---

# Questions to Answer

## 1. What is knowledge?

Define what counts as knowledge inside Ma'na.

Examples:

* facts
* claims
* observations
* interpretations
* evidence
* questions
* relationships

Which of these are fundamental?

Which are derived?

---

## 2. What is truth?

Distinguish between:

* objective fact
* historical fact
* textual fact
* interpretation
* scholarly consensus
* tradition-specific understanding
* AI inference

When should Ma'na state something as true?

When should it merely present competing claims?

---

## 3. What is the smallest atomic unit of knowledge?

Do not assume it is:

* Work
* Unit
* Poem
* Interpretation
* Claim

Discover it.

Explain why.

Everything else should be derivable from this answer.

---

## 4. What are the immutable entities?

Which concepts remain stable for decades?

Examples might include:

* Work
* Author
* Question
* Human Experience
* Symbol
* Concept
* Claim
* Source
* Tradition

Do not assume this list is correct.

Discover your own.

---

## 5. What is the purpose of literature inside Ma'na?

Is literature:

* the primary object?
* evidence?
* illustration?
* conversation?
* cultural memory?
* a carrier of human experience?

Explain your reasoning.

---

## 6. What should AI be allowed to create?

Clearly distinguish between:

* extracting
* summarizing
* synthesizing
* interpreting
* inferring
* hypothesizing

What boundaries should never be crossed?

---

## 7. What should never become canonical?

Examples:

* AI opinions
* speculative symbolism
* unsupported interpretations

Define explicit principles.

---

## 8. What are the irreducible relationships?

Discover the smallest set of relationship types required to model literature.

Avoid implementation details.

Focus on meaning.

---

## 9. What is the role of provenance?

Should every interpretation have:

* author
* source
* confidence
* evidence
* methodology

What else is essential?

---

## 10. How should disagreement exist?

Great literature invites multiple readings.

Design philosophical principles for:

* conflicting interpretations
* contradictory traditions
* evolving scholarship
* uncertainty

without collapsing them into one authoritative voice.

---

# Think Across Civilizations

Your principles must work equally well for:

* Ghalib
* Shakespeare
* Rumi
* Plato
* Dostoevsky
* Qur'an
* Bible
* Bhagavad Gita
* Confucius
* Nietzsche

No civilization should receive special treatment.

---

# Think Across Time

Assume:

* thousands of contributors
* scholars disagree
* AI becomes much smarter
* interpretations evolve
* new discoveries appear

Your principles should remain valid without redesign.

---

# Deliverable

Produce a document titled:

**First Principles of Ma'na**

Do not produce software architecture.

Do not produce schemas.

Do not produce APIs.

Do not produce databases.

Instead produce:

1. Core philosophical principles
2. Definitions
3. Invariants
4. Knowledge boundaries
5. Truth model
6. Interpretation model
7. Evidence model
8. Provenance principles
9. AI principles
10. Canonical principles

The document should be implementation-independent.

It should read like the constitution of Ma'na rather than a technical specification.

---

# Final Instruction

Challenge every assumption—including the assumptions in this prompt.

Do not preserve existing ideas simply because they have already been discussed.

If you conclude that the current vision is flawed in any way, explain why and propose a better foundation.

Your goal is not agreement.

Your goal is discovering the strongest possible first principles from which the entire Ma'na architecture can naturally emerge.
