# First Principles of Ma'na

## Purpose

Ma'na exists to build a structured map of human consciousness through literature.

Literature is not the final product.

It is the most durable and subtle record of how human beings experience longing, grief, beauty, exile, hope, terror, faith, doubt, transcendence, contradiction, and meaning.

Ma'na therefore does not treat literary works merely as texts to be searched.

It treats them as evidence-bearing carriers of human experience, reflection, symbolism, and interpretation.

The role of Ma'na is not to replace literature with summaries.

Its role is to preserve the integrity of literary works while making their meanings, tensions, interpretations, and relationships intelligible across time, traditions, and civilizations.

## Constitutional Principles

### 1. Knowledge Before Interface

Ma'na is a knowledge system before it is an application.

Any interface, workflow, storage model, or AI experience is secondary to the integrity of the knowledge it represents.

### 2. Interpretation Is Not Truth

No interpretation, however elegant, popular, or intelligent, becomes truth merely by being expressed.

Interpretations must remain distinguishable from facts, evidence, and canonical source material.

### 3. Provenance Is Mandatory

No meaningful claim may exist in Ma'na without provenance.

Every interpretation, mapping, inference, and synthesis must remain traceable to its source, method, author, and evidentiary basis.

### 4. Multiplicity Is Native

Great literature sustains multiple readings.

Ma'na must preserve coexistence, disagreement, ambiguity, and evolving scholarship without collapsing them into a single authoritative voice.

### 5. Canonical Does Not Mean Final

Canonical status in Ma'na means stable enough for reuse, reference, and coordination.

It does not mean immune to revision, challenge, or historical correction.

### 6. AI Is A Contributor, Not An Authority

AI may assist in extraction, synthesis, comparison, and hypothesis generation.

AI may never silently convert conjecture into fact or replace human responsibility for canonical judgment.

### 7. Cross-Civilizational Neutrality

No civilization, religion, language, or genre is the architectural default.

The principles of Ma'na must work equally for Ghalib, Rumi, Shakespeare, Dostoevsky, the Qur'an, Plato, Tagore, Nietzsche, Confucius, and traditions not yet represented.

### 8. The System Must Age Well

The architecture implied by these principles must survive:

- changing scholarly consensus
- multiple interpretive traditions
- billions of claims
- AI systems more capable than those that exist today
- corpora much larger than the initial Urdu starting point

## Definitions

### Knowledge

Within Ma'na, knowledge includes:

- facts
- textual observations
- interpretive claims
- relationships
- evidence
- provenance
- questions
- unresolved tensions

Knowledge is not limited to answers.

A well-preserved question may be more faithful to literature than a premature conclusion.

### Fact

A fact is a statement that Ma'na can present as true within a defined scope.

Facts may include:

- textual facts
- bibliographic facts
- historical facts
- editorial facts

A fact must always indicate the scope of its truth.

For example, a statement may be true of a specific edition, manuscript tradition, or scholarly consensus without being universally final.

### Interpretation

An interpretation is a meaning-bearing claim about a work, passage, symbol, relation, or experience.

Interpretations may be:

- traditional
- scholarly
- editorial
- reader-generated
- AI-generated

Interpretations are first-class entities in Ma'na.

They are not notes attached casually to texts.

### Evidence

Evidence is that which justifies, supports, constrains, or challenges a claim.

Evidence may be textual, historical, scholarly, comparative, or methodological.

### Provenance

Provenance is the record of where a claim came from, who or what produced it, by what method, from what evidence, under what assumptions, and at what time.

### Canonical

Canonical in Ma'na refers to a stable reference object that other knowledge can reliably point to.

Canonicality is a governance property, not a metaphysical guarantee of perfect truth.

## Invariants

The following must remain true regardless of future implementation:

### 1. Source Material Must Remain Distinct

Original works, editions, and textual witnesses must remain distinct from commentary, ontology, and AI synthesis.

### 2. Claims Must Be Separable From Evidence

No interpretive statement should be stored in a way that hides what evidence supports it.

### 3. Canonical Ontology Must Be Reusable

Themes, symbols, concepts, human experiences, and other ontology entities must exist independently of any single work.

Works may instantiate or relate to them, but should not redefine them ad hoc.

### 4. Uncertainty Must Be Representable

The system must be able to preserve ambiguity, contradiction, incompleteness, and disputed readings as first-class states.

### 5. Multiple Interpretive Traditions Must Coexist

Different scholarly, religious, literary, and philosophical traditions must be representable without forced harmonization.

### 6. AI Output Must Remain Marked

AI-generated extractions, summaries, syntheses, or hypotheses must always remain identifiable as AI-generated unless and until they are explicitly reviewed and adopted through editorial governance.

### 7. Derived Knowledge Must Be Recomputable

Anything generated through inference, synthesis, clustering, scoring, or graph traversal should remain distinguishable from source-grounded knowledge so it can be revised or recomputed.

## The Purpose of Literature Inside Ma'na

Literature is not merely content.

Within Ma'na, literature serves at least five roles simultaneously:

- primary source material
- evidence of human experience
- carrier of cultural memory
- site of interpretation and disagreement
- bridge across civilizations

Literature is therefore both object and medium.

It is an object of study, but also the medium through which human consciousness becomes knowable.

## The Smallest Atomic Unit of Knowledge

The smallest useful atomic unit of knowledge in Ma'na is not a poem, a passage, or an interpretation by itself.

It is an **anchored claim**:

> a claim connected to evidence, provenance, and scope

This is the irreducible unit because:

- a fact without scope becomes misleading
- an interpretation without evidence becomes opinion
- evidence without a claim is inert
- provenance without a claim has nothing to govern

Works, passages, commentary documents, and knowledge packages are all higher-order assemblies built from anchored claims and their relationships.

## Immutable Entities

The following are the strongest candidates for long-lived architectural entities:

- Source
- Work
- Witness or Edition
- Segment or Addressable Span
- Claim
- Evidence
- Provenance Record
- Contributor
- Tradition
- Ontology Entity
- Relationship
- Question

These are not equal in importance, but they are more fundamental than UI-oriented concepts such as pages, filters, cards, or chat responses.

## Truth Model

Ma'na must distinguish among different kinds of truth.

### 1. Textual Truth

What does a specific source or edition contain?

This is the most stable kind of truth in the system.

### 2. Historical Truth

What is historically supported by evidence and scholarship?

This remains revisable and scoped.

### 3. Interpretive Truth

What does a tradition, commentator, or reader argue a passage means?

This is not universal truth.

It is truth within an interpretive framework.

### 4. Consensus Truth

What is broadly accepted by a community of scholars or a tradition?

Consensus should be representable without erasing minority views.

### 5. Inferential Truth

What has been inferred by AI or analytical process?

Inferential truth must remain explicitly marked as inferential and never pass as settled fact by default.

### When Ma'na May State Something As True

Ma'na may state something as true only when:

- the claim type permits truth-status
- the scope is explicit
- the evidence is sufficient
- provenance is present
- no stronger competing status is being hidden

Otherwise, Ma'na should present the matter as a competing claim, unresolved question, or interpretation.

## Interpretation Model

Interpretation is central to Ma'na, but it must remain disciplined.

### Interpretations Must Be Modeled, Not Implied

Every interpretation should indicate:

- what is being interpreted
- by whom or by what
- from which tradition or method
- with what evidence
- with what degree of confidence or contestability

### Interpretations May Conflict

Conflicting interpretations are not corruption.

They are often the proper state of literary knowledge.

### Interpretations Must Not Collapse Levels

Ma'na must distinguish:

- paraphrase
- explanation
- symbolic reading
- emotional reading
- philosophical reading
- mystical reading
- comparative reading
- AI synthesis

These may converge, but they must not be stored as if they are the same kind of thing.

## Evidence Model

Evidence is the constraint that protects Ma'na from becoming a stylistic opinion engine.

Evidence may include:

- directly quoted textual span
- linguistic observation
- vocabulary history
- editorial note
- historical source
- scholarly commentary
- parallel passage
- interpretive tradition

Claims unsupported by any identifiable evidence must remain non-canonical and explicitly speculative.

## Provenance Principles

Every meaningful claim should carry enough provenance to answer:

- who made it
- what type of contributor made it
- when it was made
- what source it used
- what method produced it
- what evidence it relied on
- whether it was reviewed
- under which version of the knowledge model it was produced

Provenance is not metadata decoration.

It is the governance layer of knowledge.

## AI Principles

### What AI May Do

AI may:

- extract candidates from source material
- summarize existing commentary
- synthesize across multiple approved claims
- compare works and traditions
- identify patterns worth human review
- generate hypotheses
- assist in explanation

### What AI Must Not Do Automatically

AI must not:

- invent facts and present them as canonical
- merge conflicting traditions silently
- erase uncertainty for fluency
- convert weak evidence into authoritative interpretation
- decide canonical ontology unilaterally
- present speculation as consensus

### AI Must Preserve Auditability

Any AI contribution meaningful enough to influence retrieval, commentary, ontology, or graph relationships must remain inspectable and reversible.

## Canonical Principles

The following should never become canonical merely through repetition or convenience:

- AI opinions
- unsupported symbolism
- unattributed commentary
- speculative cross-civilizational equivalences
- inferred ontology mappings without review
- stylistically persuasive but ungrounded explanation

Canonicality should require explicit governance, not rhetorical confidence.

## Irreducible Relationships

The smallest family of meaning-bearing relationships in Ma'na should be philosophical before technical.

At minimum, the system must be able to express:

- originates from
- refers to
- interprets
- supports
- challenges
- resembles
- contrasts with
- belongs to
- evokes
- derives from
- is evidenced by

These relationship types may later expand, but Ma'na should begin with the smallest set that preserves meaning rather than proliferating edges for convenience.

## Disagreement Principles

Disagreement is not a bug in literary knowledge.

It is often the material itself.

Therefore:

- disagreement must be preservable
- contradictory readings may coexist
- traditions may disagree without one being erased
- editorial approval must never imply universal truth
- uncertainty should be represented honestly

Ma'na should aim for faithful plurality, not artificial closure.

## Knowledge Boundaries

Ma'na must clearly distinguish among the following layers:

- source material
- editorial segmentation
- factual description
- interpretation
- ontology mapping
- graph relationship
- AI synthesis
- user-facing explanation

These layers may interact, but they must not be conflated.

If they collapse into one another, the system loses both scholarly integrity and long-term maintainability.

## Non-Goals

Ma'na is not trying to become:

- a generic quote database
- a flat metadata catalog
- a one-answer explanation engine
- a keyword search engine dressed as AI
- a system where AI fluency replaces evidence
- a platform that forces all traditions into one synthetic worldview

## Consequences For Future Design

Any future schema, ontology, graph model, editorial workflow, retrieval pipeline, or AI interface should be evaluated against these questions:

- Does it preserve the distinction between source and interpretation?
- Does it preserve provenance?
- Does it allow disagreement?
- Does it represent uncertainty honestly?
- Does it keep AI contributions auditable?
- Does it support cross-civilizational neutrality?
- Can it scale without changing these principles?

If the answer to any of these is no, then the design violates the constitution of Ma'na.

## Final Principle

Ma'na should not aim merely to store literature.

It should aim to preserve the conditions under which literature can continue to illuminate human consciousness without being reduced, flattened, or falsely simplified by the systems that process it.
