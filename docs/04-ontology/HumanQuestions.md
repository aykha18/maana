# HumanQuestions

Human questions are the highest-level discovery layer in the ontology.

They represent the universal questions that people bring to poetry, whether or not
they can articulate them directly.

## Purpose

Human questions allow the platform to connect poems across languages, civilizations,
religions, and literary traditions through shared inquiry rather than shared wording.

## Examples

### Love

- What is love?
- Can love survive separation?
- Is longing part of love?

### Suffering

- Why do humans suffer?
- Does suffering reveal truth?
- Can suffering transform the self?

### Faith

- Why does God feel distant?
- What does devotion require?
- Can doubt belong to faith?

### Death

- What survives death?
- Why do humans fear death?
- Is mortality a curse, a teacher, or a threshold?

### Time

- Can the past be recovered?
- What does memory preserve?
- Is time a wound or a form of mercy?

### Freedom

- Are we truly free?
- What binds the self?
- Can liberation happen inwardly before outwardly?

## Why This Layer Matters

- Questions unify human inquiry across traditions.
- Experiences describe what people feel.
- Themes describe what poems discuss.
- Symbols describe how poems express meaning.

Without the question layer, the ontology risks starting too low in the stack.

## Example Chain

- Human question: Why do humans fear death?
- Human experience: dread
- Theme: mortality
- Symbol: dust
- Poem: a poem by Ghalib, Dickinson, or Rumi

## Rule

A human question should be broad, durable, and cross-civilizational.
It should not be so narrow that it only describes one poem or one editorial note.
