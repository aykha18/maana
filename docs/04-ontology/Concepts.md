# Concepts

Concepts are abstract ideas that connect themes, symbols, human experiences, and commentary.

## Purpose

Concepts give the ontology a layer between raw literary content and broad discovery.
They help normalize ideas that appear in many forms across poets, languages, and traditions.

## Examples

- absence
- memory
- ego
- surrender
- transcendence
- mortality

## Why Concepts Matter

- Themes often depend on concepts.
- Symbols often point toward concepts.
- Commentary frequently explains concepts rather than only text.
- Search and recommendations need a stable conceptual layer.

## Example Mapping

- Symbol: wine
- Concept: intoxication
- Concept: release
- Concept: divine ecstasy

- Symbol: dust
- Concept: mortality
- Concept: humility
- Concept: impermanence

## Rule

If an idea repeatedly appears across poems and affects interpretation, search, or comparison,
it should likely be modeled as a concept.

## Reader Preparation Role

Some concepts should also be eligible to act as foundations for understanding.

Examples:

- impermanence
- transcendence
- ego
- alienation

This means the product may use concepts not only for ontology and search, but also for
readiness guidance and short lessons.
