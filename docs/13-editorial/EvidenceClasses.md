# EvidenceClasses

Every substantive editorial claim should be attached to an evidence class.

This is how the platform distinguishes observation, interpretation, inherited scholarship,
and editorial synthesis.

## Purpose

Evidence classes make the platform more trustworthy and make disagreements easier to manage.

Instead of only storing the statement:

`Wine symbolizes divine ecstasy.`

the system should also know what kind of evidence supports that statement.

## Canonical Evidence Classes

### Original Text

The claim is supported directly by the poem's wording, structure, or syntax.

### Translator Interpretation

The claim depends on how a translator has rendered the text or explained a choice.

### Classical Commentary

The claim is grounded in a traditional commentary source or inherited interpretive tradition.

### Modern Scholarly Opinion

The claim comes from modern academic criticism, scholarship, or critical editions.

### Editorial Synthesis

The claim is a responsible editorial conclusion drawn from multiple sources and internal analysis.

## Rule

Editorial synthesis is allowed, but it should never disguise itself as direct textual evidence
or inherited scholarly consensus.

## Suggested Usage

Each commentary or annotation claim should be able to store:

- claim text
- evidence class
- source reference
- confidence note when needed
- competing interpretation if relevant
