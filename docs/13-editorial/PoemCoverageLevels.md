# PoemCoverageLevels

Not every poem needs the same editorial depth at the same time.

Coverage levels define how much of the canonical poem structure has been completed.
This allows the library to grow incrementally without pretending that all poems are equally mature.

## Level 1

Basic reading layer.

- original text
- poem identity metadata
- at least one translation or transliteration

## Level 2

Core learning layer.

- level 1 requirements
- word meanings for key terms
- basic ontology tags such as themes and human experiences

## Level 3

Interpretive layer.

- level 2 requirements
- symbols
- historical context
- literary commentary

## Level 4

Deep reading layer.

- level 3 requirements
- mystical commentary where relevant
- philosophical commentary where relevant
- stronger relationship mapping

## Level 5

Fully interconnected layer.

- level 4 requirements
- cross-civilization comparisons
- scholarly references
- linked reference articles
- well-developed evidence and citation records

## Rule

Coverage level describes breadth of completion.
It does not necessarily describe the quality of what has been written.
